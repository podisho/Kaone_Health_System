Patient Rights

Patient rights are fundamental to ensuring that individuals receive respectful, ethical, and high-quality healthcare.

Key Patient Rights

    • 1.Right to Respect and Dignity: Patients have the right to be treated with respect, courtesy, and dignity at all times
      2.Right to Informed Consent: Patients have the right to receive clear, comprehensive information about their diagnosis, treatment options, and potential risks, and to make informed decisions about their care
      3.Right to Privacy and Confidentiality: Patients have the right to have their medical information kept confidential and to have their privacy respected during medical examinations and treatments
      4.Right to Access Medical Records: Patients have the right to access their medical records and request corrections if necessary
      5.Right to Refuse Treatment: Patients have the right to refuse medical treatment or procedures, and to be informed of the potential consequences of doing so
      6.Right to Emergency Care: Patients have the right to receive emergency medical care regardless of their ability to pay
      7.Right to Non-Discrimination: Patients have the right to receive medical care without discrimination based on race, gender, religion, nationality, or any other characteristic
      8.Right to Participate in Decision-Making: Patients have the right to participate in decisions about their healthcare, including the right to seek a second opinion
      9.Right to a Safe Environment: Patients have the right to a safe and clean healthcare environment
      10.Right to Complaints and Redress: Patients have the right to voice complaints about their care and to have those complaints addressed in a timely and effective manner
